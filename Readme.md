### About

I build systems that think and act independently.

I've got two AI agents running 24/7 on hardware I own. They coordinate tasks, pull in data, call tools, and make decisions on their own. No one's babysitting them. I built the agent runtime myself, and the whole thing runs in production — not as a side project I demo at meetups.

Before any of this, I spent 5+ years in fintech and enterprise engineering. Authorization platforms handling 5,000+ TPS under 200ms SLA, PCI-compliant microservices, ML pipelines, the usual distributed systems headaches. Environments where if something breaks at 2AM, your phone rings. That shaped how I think about building AI systems too — observability, fallback logic, structured decision-making. None of that is optional when your agents are supposed to run unsupervised.

The thing I keep coming back to is trust. Not "can an agent do something smart once" but "can it keep doing the right thing when nobody's looking." That's a harder problem than most people realize, and it's mostly an infrastructure problem, not a model problem.
